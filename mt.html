Porridge

with Milk and Eggs

-1 cup finger millet or sorghum flour: Provides fiber, iron, and other essential nutrients.

-4 cups water: For cooking the porridge.

-1 cup milk: Adds calcium, protein, and creaminess to the porridge.

-1 egg: A source of protein and essential amino acids.

-1 tablespoon sugar or honey (optional): For added sweetness (use sparingly).

-A pinch of salt (optional)

1.Create a slurry by mixing millet or sorghum flour with cold water.

2.Heat 4 cups of water in a saucepan.

3.Cook the porridge by adding the slurry, milk, and egg.

4.Cook for 10-15 minutes, adding salt if needed.

5.Beat the egg in a separate bowl and add it to the porridge.

6.Optionally, add sugar or honey.

7.Serve hot and garnish with cinnamon or nuts.
SNACK

Yoghurt

with Fruit

Plain yoghurt: 1 cup (about 250 ml)

Fresh fruit: Choose any combination of fruits available locally, such as:

-1 ripe banana (sliced)

-1/4 cup pineapple chunks

-1/4 cup papaya (cubed)

-1/4 cup watermelon (diced)

-Other options: mango, apple, or guava

-Honey (optional): 1 teaspoon for added sweetness

-Optional toppings: A sprinkle of roasted groundnuts or chia seeds for added texture and protein

1.Prepare the Fruit: Wash, peel, and slice the fruits into small, bite-sized pieces.

2.Mix with Yoghurt: In a bowl, add the plain yoghurt and stir in the fruit pieces.

3.Optional Toppings: Sprinkle a few roasted groundnuts or chia seeds for added crunch and nutrition.

4.Serve: Eat immediately or chill for a few minutes if you prefer it extra cold.

SNACK

Popcorn

-2–3 tablespoons of vegetable oil (such as sunflower or corn oil)

-1/3 cup of popcorn kernels

-Salt, to taste

1.Heat the Oil: In a large, heavy-bottomed pot, add the oil and heat it over medium heat. Drop in 3–4 popcorn kernels and cover with a lid.

2.Wait for Popping: Once those few kernels pop, you’ll know the oil is hot enough.

3.Add the Kernels: Add the rest of the kernels in an even layer on the bottom of the pot. Cover the pot and remove it from heat for about 30 seconds.

4.Return to Heat: Place the pot back on the heat. Keep the lid slightly ajar to let steam escape, which will help keep the popcorn crispy.

5.Shake the Pot: Gently shake the pot occasionally as the kernels pop. Once the popping slows to about 2 seconds between pops, remove the pot from heat.

6.Season: Add salt to taste, or try some of the flavoring ideas below.

Green Lentil Stew with Spinach

-1 cup green lentils, rinsed

-1 tablespoon cooking oil (such as sunflower or vegetable oil)

-1 medium onion, chopped

-2 cloves garlic, minced

-1 large tomato, chopped

-1-2 carrots, diced

-1 teaspoon cumin powder (optional, for extra flavor)

-1 teaspoon salt (or to taste)

-1/2 teaspoon black pepper

-1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder

-2-3 cups water or vegetable broth

-2 cups fresh spinach, roughly chopped

-Fresh coriander leaves for garnish (optional)

1.Rinse lentils, sauté onions and garlic, add vegetables and spices, and cook until soft.

2.Cook lentils in vegetable broth, cover, and simmer for 25-30 minutes.

3.Add spinach and cook until wilted. Season with salt and coriander, and serve hot.
